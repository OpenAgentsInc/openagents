Floating compute infrastructure

A 1000 meter ocean tower for compute, minerals, and new cities.

Ocean Tower Compute is an investable infrastructure platform: modular data centers funded by advantaged cooling and power, with mineral-rich seawater and livable offshore real estate as expansion rights.

See the tower Investment case

The core geometry is simple: a roughly 40 meter diameter tower extending about 1000 meters below the surface, with an inner cold seawater pipe of about 20 meters diameter and a surrounding ring for servers, energy systems, and service access.

Mostly below water. Built for the equatorial ocean.

The tower uses the ocean column as both machine room and climate system. Warm surface water drives OTEC. Cold deep seawater rises through the central pipe. Servers occupy the structural annulus, where cooling, power conversion, and maintenance paths can be packaged into repeatable modules.

A compute site that gets more valuable as it learns the ocean.

The first product is resilient offshore compute. The larger investment case compounds through desalination, chilled-water services, seawater mineral extraction, aquaculture adjacency, and livable territory in international waters.

Power and cooling in one place

OTEC turns thermal gradient into electricity while SWAC uses deep cold water to reduce data center cooling load.

Environmental upside

Returning and aerating deep seawater can circulate minerals and support productive ocean systems when governed carefully.

Mineral stream optionality

Seawater processing can become a parallel resource business as the tower matures and water handling scales.

Known physics, new packaging

The opportunity is packaging known ocean systems into a financeable platform.

Existing OTEC demonstrations show the energy loop. Ocean Tower Compute turns that loop into a repeatable asset class by adding a structural tower, a large central intake, modular compute bays, and above-water real estate that can evolve from operations base to settlement.

The build path starts narrow and compounds.

The near-term job is not to explain every engineering detail. It is to prove a credible wedge: constructible tower, validated water loop, compute-grade cooling, and a capital path from pilot platform to repeatable offshore city module.

  1. Prototype the tower section

    Validate UHPC, geopolymer, airform, and shotcrete methods for repeatable structural segments and internal service geometry.

  2. Demonstrate cold-water handling

    Prove the intake pipe, pumping profile, thermal transfer, and return-water stewardship with a monitoring plan.

  3. Attach modular compute

    Start with a contained compute payload, then expand power, cooling, and mineral processing modules as economics improve.

  4. Grow into ocean settlement

    Add housing, tourism, research, and governance capacity above the waterline before repeating the tower as a network.
