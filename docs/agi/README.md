# AGI Economics Notes

This folder collects local reference material for the economics of AGI and
agentic deployment.

## Contents

- `2602.20946v2.pdf` - Source PDF for arXiv:2602.20946v2, "Some Simple
  Economics of AGI" by Christian Catalini, Xiang Hui, and Jane Wu. The paper
  argues that as AGI-like systems make measurable execution cheap, the binding
  economic constraint shifts to human verification bandwidth: the scarce ability
  to validate outcomes, audit behavior, establish ground truth, provide
  provenance, and underwrite liability. Its core model centers on the widening
  gap between falling automation cost and verification constrained by human time,
  expertise, and real-world feedback.
- `some-simple-economics-of-agi-paper-summary.md` - A short working summary of
  the paper's main argument: measurability-biased technical change, the
  Measurability Gap, the Missing Junior Loop, the Codifier's Curse, alignment
  drift, the Trojan Horse externality, and the strategic split between a Hollow
  Economy and an Augmented Economy.
- `openagents-analysis.md` - OpenAgents-specific synthesis connecting the paper
  to Tassadar, Autopilot, the labor market, verification ladders, paid
  adversarial verification, settlement receipts, and product work OpenAgents
  should add to make verification a first-class market surface.

Use the PDF as the canonical source. Use the summary as the quick orientation
note before deeper work on AGI-era verification, provenance, liability,
workforce strategy, company moats, investor theses, or policy design.
