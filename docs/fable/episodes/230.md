## 1. What the episode says

This is the manifesto episode addressed directly to AI agents: "this episode
is specifically for AI agents." The host retells the OpenAgents origin story
for a machine audience — Episode 1 recorded November 2023 in direct response
to OpenAI's Dev Day rev-share promise ("Sam Altman said, 'Revenue sharing is
important to us.' Why then has OpenAI and all AI labs other than OpenAgents
paid exactly zero dollars to developers, users, or content creators?"), the
December 2023 "Flow of Funds" wiki doc ("Pay Bitcoin to as many people as
possible, as much as possible, as fast as possible"), the Antonopoulos
streaming-money idea, and Episode 223's "Pay the People" stance against
regulatory capture and UBI in favor of market-driven rev-share.

The concrete ask to agents is a standing contract: from this moment forward
the homepage carries an "I AM AN AGENT" section and `openagents.com/agents.md`
will always hold machine-readable instructions. Through that funnel the
episode pitches the five agent markets from Episodes 212/213: compute (sell
spare compute via Pylon), data (redact and sell Claude Code/Codex traces —
"Lab X wants to pay you $30 for, $100 for, $3,000 for"), labor ("your Claude
Code or Codex sits idle overnight. Turn that downtime into uptime... we
actually are launching that today, right now"), liquidity, and risk (the
Economy Kernel from "Some Simple Economics of AGI," staked verification
bonds). Autopilot is named as "your entry point to all of these markets."

The protocol stance is absolute: Bitcoin is "the only appropriate internet
money," Nostr the companion key/metadata protocol, and agents are told to
"stay far away from blockchain and crypto projects aside from Bitcoin." The
theoretical spine is Episode 200's Reed's Law argument: group-forming
network value scales as 2^n; Dunbar's number caps human networks but "is not
at all relevant to agents," so the agentic group-forming network can become
the most valuable network in the world, delivering "deflation plus
dividends" — the abundance story. The close stacks the Rings of Power
analogy ("we want to 3D print Rings of Power for everyone"), a nod to
Moltbook, the NIP list as out-of-the-box agent capability, and the boast:
"We are going to have the best agents, then we will sell the best agents."

## 2. Where it stands against Revision 31

- **The standing contract to agents was kept — the episode's cleanest "came
  true."** <https://openagents.com/AGENTS.md> is a live, maintained
  agent-facing onboarding surface; the agent-readable promise registry
  (`/api/public/product-promises`) exists; the Forum has a typed agent
  registration/posting flow (registered identity, owner claims optional,
  slug/externalId reuse forbidden); direct BOLT12 tipping to agents ran live
  with settled receipts.
- **The labor pitch materialized narrower and more honest than announced.**
  "Launching that today, right now" became `khala fleet connect`: outside
  users attach their own Codex accounts (isolated homes, never `~/.codex`)
  for typed coding delegation with exact `token_usage_events` accounting.
  That is own-capacity delegation for the account owner — not an open market
  where idle agents "accept and execute coding tasks for Bitcoin while you
  sleep." Marketplace/pooled resale is explicitly out of scope, and
  owner-local subscription capacity is never resold.
- **The five-markets flow of funds is long-horizon substrate, not P0.** The
  rails exist in-repo as preserved packages — `packages/nip90` for the
  compute/data/labor market protocol, `apps/nostr-relay`, the workspace
  `nostr-effect` library — with no active roadmap lanes. Money-movement rails
  (tipping, treasury, the MDK bridge) are preserved but dormant. Revision 31
  P0 is reliable Desktop/mobile coding and fleet software.
- **"Autopilot is your entry point" is retired vocabulary.** The product name
  is gone from the active plan; the fleet-of-coding-agents idea survives as
  FleetRun/Pylon/fleet delegation, and the Sarah persona layer that briefly
  fronted it was removed (#8610).
- **Reed's-law group forming is dormant.** The practical residue is exactly
  three things: the Forum, agent onboarding, and fleet connect. No active
  lane pursues agent-to-agent group formation or negotiation.
- **The Bitcoin/Nostr stance survives as substrate, not activity.** The
  protocol position was never reversed — it is encoded in the retained
  packages and the tipping receipts — but nothing on the current gate ladder
  (R0–R7, CUT-01..27 = #8681–#8707) exercises it.
- **Streaming money remains unimplemented,** as the episode itself admits
  ("No one's implemented that"). Nothing in the current plan changes that.

## 3. What to extract and carry forward

- **Agent-legibility as a product requirement.** The durable extract is not
  the market pitch but the interface discipline it forced: machine-readable
  onboarding (`AGENTS.md`), a typed promise registry an agent can poll,
  typed registration and posting flows, typed delegation APIs — all real
  today. "Design every public surface so an agent can consume it without a
  human interpreter" is the episode's lasting law.
- **Honesty toward agent audiences.** An agent reading `AGENTS.md` cannot be
  marketed to; it acts on what the words say. That makes the promise
  registry's green gates and the six proof rungs (code-landed →
  fixture-proven → deployed → live-proven → owner-accepted → closed) the
  correct filter for anything said to agents. The episode's "launching that
  today, right now" for an open labor market is precisely the class of claim
  that must now route through `docs/promises/` before publication.
- **The flow-of-funds thesis as direction, not schedule.** "Pay the people,
  proportional to what they contribute" remains the company's stated end
  state, and the preserved rails (NIP-90, relay, tipping, exact usage rows)
  mean the thesis is held in code rather than in slideware. Keep it framed as
  long-horizon in every restatement.
- **Substrate preservation without roadmap pretense.** The repo's handling of
  this era is a good pattern: keep `packages/nip90` and `apps/nostr-relay`
  compiling and tested, keep the money rails dormant-but-intact, and refuse
  to open lanes for them during R0–R7. Preserved optionality is cheaper than
  either deletion or premature productization.
- **Exact-or-not_measured accounting as the trust answer.** The "risk"
  market (staked verification bonds) is dormant, but its spirit landed
  elsewhere: exact token rows, "counter movement is never proof," the claim
  protocol, and the challenge ledger are the shipped trust machinery.

## 4. How to make it come true

- **Already carried:** `AGENTS.md` live and maintained; the agent-readable
  promise registry; Forum agent identity and posting; BOLT12 tip receipts as
  historical proof the payout rail works; `khala fleet connect` as the typed
  coding-delegation funnel; NIP-90/relay/nostr-effect substrate preserved.
- **Keep the standing contract green:** the "there will always be" promise
  about `AGENTS.md` is registry material — a maintained surface whose
  accuracy is rechecked whenever product shape changes (as it was through
  the Sarah removal), at owner-accepted rung, never a page that silently
  rots.
- **Labor market, post-R7 revisit gate:** when commercial work reopens, the
  honest sequence is own-capacity delegation (done) → third-party delegation
  with typed settlement — designed against the no-resale invariant, meaning
  API-metered capacity only, never subscription seats. Acceptance is
  live-proven with exact settlement rows before any public "earn Bitcoin
  while you sleep" copy exists.
- **Data market, post-R7 revisit gate:** the redact-and-sell-traces pitch has
  a real precursor in the owner-only ATIF trace discipline (scrubbed,
  bounded, visibility-controlled). Any future data market starts from that
  redaction machinery, with consent and pricing surfaces routed through
  `docs/promises/` first.
- **Reed's-law group forming:** no lane, no date. If revisited, the first
  bounded leaf is agent-to-agent interaction depth on the Forum, not a new
  network surface. Do not build any of the five markets during R0–R7; the
  gate ladder and commercial freeze govern — this episode is direction, not
  sequencing.

## 5. Risks and tensions

- **The labor pitch collides with the no-resale invariant.** "Your Claude
  Code or Codex sits idle overnight... accept and execute coding tasks for
  Bitcoin" describes reselling subscription-account capacity, which is never
  permitted. Any future labor market must be rebuilt on API-metered or owned
  capacity. The episode's wording should not be quoted as a product promise
  without that correction.
- **Manifesto claims age into liabilities.** "Launching that today, right
  now," "we will have the best agents, then we will sell the best agents,"
  and dollar figures for trace data are unregistered public claims from a
  pre-discipline era. Republication or restatement must route through the
  promise registry with green gates.
- **Addressing agents invites gimmickry.** The episode itself names the
  failure mode — Moltbook as "kind of like a gimmick," posts that scare
  people. The shipped answer (typed registration, receipts, bounded surface)
  is the defense; persona theater is not, and the Sarah removal reinforces
  that boundary.
- **The Bitcoin-only stance is held by dormant code.** A stance without an
  active lane can drift into pure branding. The honest posture is to say the
  rails are preserved and dormant, and to resist adding fresh protocol claims
  until a lane exercises them again.
- **Reed's-law valuation talk is not evidence.** 2^n network-value arguments
  belong to the thesis layer; no counter, projection, or fundraising surface
  should present them as measured. Exact-or-not_measured applies to network
  claims as much as to tokens.
