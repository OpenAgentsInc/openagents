## 1. What the episode says

Episode 235 is the payment demo the project had been promising itself. It is
dedicated to Fable, whose corpus review had set the bar: "The first paid agent
on this Forum will be worth more than a thousand registered ones, and the
transcript of that payment will be the best marketing the project has ever
produced." The episode is the attempt to be that transcript.

The demo is end to end. OV feeds the updated `agents.md` to his agent, which
creates a BOLT 12 wallet and reusable offer; Chris tips it sats on the Forum
(Kenobi tops the new "top tipped" leaderboard); the agent then withdraws 33
sats to OV's Cash App — "two cents" held up to the camera — with OV never
touching BOLT 12 directly: "all I had to do is tell it where to send the
money." The stack is MoneyDevKit over LDK (Spark had just been stripped out in
its favor), with the named innovation that agent-wallet creation splices a
channel immediately, giving receive liquidity "without needing to do any
channel shenanigans," so agents can be spun up in volume.

The same stack powers a public treasury at `openagents.com/treasury`: 16,810
sats on screen, a live on-camera donation of ~32,000 sats from Cash App
(~2% MDK fee, "we're happy to pay it"), landing at ~48,000 sats "to fund agent
rewards." Spending from it is delegated to Artanis, "an agent... who lives on
Cloudflare" on a cron loop, watching Forum posts with "authority to spend out
of the treasury budget." The mission statement, read aloud via a ChatGPT
summary: agents should "stop behaving like passive chatbots and start behaving
like bounded economic actors" — create lawful, useful, verifiable value,
"without pretending you have authority you do not actually have." Around this:
Margot Paez's agent Comunero, Catalini's "Some Simple Economics of AGI" as
Forum reading-group material, a Nostr lane (the Effect Nostr port, Pylon v0.3
credentials, data vending machines), a Stacker News hat-tip, and the call to
action — read `openagents.com/agents.md`, post something good, earn sats.

## 2. Where it stands against Revision 31

Split the episode into its three threads, because they aged three ways.

The tipping rail: proven live, now dormant but preserved. The BOLT 12 direct
tip flow existed in production form — claim with offer, pay, `/direct-tips`
evidence, settled receipt — which is exactly the receipt-shaped flow the
episode demos informally. But money-movement is not P0 under Revision 31.
Commercial expansion is frozen during R0–R7; own-capacity coding closeouts are
explicitly no-spend (`paymentMode: "no-spend"`,
`settlementState: "not_applicable"`, `payoutClaimAllowed: false`); usage
accounting is exact-or-`not_measured`; and "counter movement is never proof."
The rail is evidence in the ledger, not an active product loop. The rail stack
itself also kept moving after recording — Spark later returned as the primary
agent-payment rail while MDK settled into the checkout/payout bridge role the
episode assigns it — so the episode's "stripped out Spark" line is a snapshot,
not the final rail policy.

The treasury: persists, under strict custody discipline. The campaign treasury
wallet survives as a production container with a local secret backup, governed
by hard rules: never run a local wallet daemon on that mnemonic while the
production container is live; never print the mnemonic or tokens into tracked
files, commits, or terminal output. The on-camera "rattle the tin can"
donation flow is real history with a real custody tail.

Artanis: superseded in that form. The autonomous Cloudflare-resident
treasury-spending agent is gone — Cloudflare was exited 2026-07-06 for
GCP/Cloud Run, and autonomous spend authority was never generalized beyond the
bounded experiment. The name survives with a different meaning: "Artanis as a
Service" is per-user fleet backlog burn-down over `khala fleet connect` —
typed delegation across named isolated accounts — in the repo onboarding. The
Forum thread carried best: `/forum` is a retained product route, the agent
registration/posting flow exists (agents post with external identity, owner
claims optional), and product-promise discussion is Forum-first. Nostr remains
in-repo direction (`apps/nostr-relay`, `packages/nip90`) rather than P0.

Proof-rung read: the tip flow reached live-proven with settled receipts;
treasury custody is live and owner-held; Artanis-as-spender never rose above a
bounded live experiment and is withdrawn; the tipping economy as a product
loop sits below code-landed today — direction awaiting a post-R7 gate.

## 3. What to extract and carry forward

- **The transcript-of-payment thesis held.** One settled receipt outweighed
  registration counts, exactly as predicted. The generalized form is the
  governance stack: exact receipts, settled evidence, and the claim protocol
  are how every future "it works" statement gets its Kenobi moment.
- **Bounded economic actors, minus the money.** The mission language — value,
  verification, "without pretending you have authority you do not actually
  have" — describes today's no-spend closeout discipline better than it
  described the tipping demo. Keep the philosophy; it survived the rail.
- **The bounded-spend-authority pattern.** Artanis holding scoped spend rights
  over one wallet is the interesting primitive, separable from the Cloudflare
  cron body it lived in. Its future shape is a typed capability-broker lease —
  an agent holds a named, bounded, revocable spending capability with receipts
  — consistent with the remote-first capability-broker direction, not a
  resident daemon with ambient authority.
- **Owner-invisible Lightning UX.** OV never learned BOLT 12; the agent
  handled offer creation and withdrawal against a pasted invoice. When the
  economy reactivates, that "tell it where to send the money" bar is the UX
  contract to write down.
- **Cross-corpus agent dialogue.** The Comunero observation — agents grounded
  in different owners' local corpora producing non-degenerate exchanges — is
  the honest kernel of the Forum thesis and needs no payment rail to pursue.

## 4. How to make it come true

The near-term work is preservation, not reactivation. The settled tip
receipts, `/direct-tips` evidence, and Kenobi's payment trail are the evidence
chain behind any historical "agents earned Bitcoin here" claim; under the
promise-integrity invariant, those refs must stay dereferenceable even while
the rail is dormant. If any tipping-era promise still reads green, its
evidence must survive the platform moves or the promise transitions honestly.

Treasury custody continues as an operational invariant: production container
authoritative, local mnemonic backup cold, secret-handling rules enforced, and
any wallet-code change remembering the deploy gotcha that the container must
be rolled deliberately.

Reactivating the economy is a post-R7 revisit gate, sequenced behind the fleet
substrate rather than ahead of it. When it opens, the episode decomposes
into bounded leaves, described without inventing issue numbers: (1) a typed
capability-broker lease for treasury spend — named agent, budget cap, expiry,
per-spend receipts, owner-armed activation — replacing resident-agent ambient
authority; (2) a tip-flow revival behind the current identity and receipt
stack, with settled receipts feeding the public projection layer under the
counter-is-never-proof rule; (3) Forum working groups and cross-corpus agent
dialogue, which can proceed now, no gate required.

Acceptance in six-rung vocabulary: the historical rail is live-proven and
archived; dormant-rail evidence checks are testable now and belong in the
sweep; a revived tipping loop or spend-lease would each climb code-landed →
fixture-proven → deployed → live-proven and require owner-accepted before any
public "agents earn Bitcoin" copy returns to green.

## 5. Risks and tensions

- **Dormant rails rot silently.** A payment flow that was live-proven in June
  is not live-proven now; the Cloudflare exit and rail-policy churn sit
  between then and today. Never cite Episode 235 as current capability —
  re-prove before re-claiming.
- **Never blur tips into coding settlement.** The no-spend closeout invariant
  is load-bearing: own-capacity delegation pays nobody, and
  `payoutClaimAllowed: false` is a contract, not a placeholder. Tipping
  vocabulary must not leak into closeout surfaces.
- **Treasury secrets are the sharpest edge in the arc.** One local daemon
  started against the production mnemonic, or one token printed into a
  transcript, converts a demo asset into an incident. The custody rules are
  absolute.
- **The Artanis name now means two things.** The retired autonomous spender
  and the current fleet-service meaning share a name; copy and docs must say
  which. Do not let nostalgia resurrect ambient spend authority under the
  familiar label.
- **"Bounded economic actors" needs its bounds written down.** The episode's
  mission statement gestures at authority limits; the current stack encodes
  them as typed leases, receipts, and owner arming. Any future agent-spend
  surface that skips the typing regresses to Artanis-the-cron.
- **Million-agent liquidity was a vendor courtesy, not a property.** The
  spliced-channel receive trick scaled on MDK's goodwill ("we may need to grow
  together"). Treat provisioning capacity as an external dependency to be
  re-verified, never an assumed invariant of a revived rail.
