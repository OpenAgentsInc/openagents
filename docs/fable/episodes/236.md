## 1. What the episode says

A very short launch announcement, filmed outdoors in New York heat, that
exists to set a date and a name: "Monday we are launching the largest
decentralized training run we think ever in history." The number to beat is
named — "like 200 contributors" from Bittensor — and the mechanism for beating
it is stated in one sentence: "you are just going to install our node software
and you are going to get paid Bitcoin to contribute to a training run."

Three decisions are disclosed. First, the model question: the team went back
and forth between "a pre-trained model from scratch, like a tiny language
model, or a fine tune of a Llama model," with a wink at Anthropic along the
way ("they are like 'oh we do not help you with distributed training.' Well...
you did. You were very, very helpful"). Second, the architecture: the
"Percepta Executor Class model" — "CPU computation transformed... a new
frontier" — will be supported in Pylon version 0.3. Third, the packaging:
Pylon v0.3 mashes together the training support, "the Bitcoin payments,"
"the forum and the coding agents and stuff" into "one piece of software
that is going to earn you Bitcoin in a multiple, multiple different ways."

The model gets its name in the closing seconds: "this very experimental but
we think very powerful new kind of model which we are calling Tassadar.
Tassadar, okay? Uh, we'll launch that Monday, so enjoy your weekend."

## 2. Where it stands against Revision 31

- **The training run it announces is retired.** Tassadar, Psionic, and
  distributed training left the active plan in the 2026-07-08 docs-direction
  cleanup. `docs/tassadar/` is retained as the research record, and the
  machine-work economy remains long-horizon vision, not P0. The episode is
  the setup beat for 237's constitutional launch. Both now read as historical
  record of a lane deliberately banked.
- **Pylon survived, but not as a training node.** The actual descendant of
  "install our node software" is `apps/pylon` as the contributor/local
  execution runtime powering typed Khala coding delegation (`pylon khala
  request --workflow codex_agent_task`), with named isolated accounts and
  no-spend closeouts (`paymentMode: no-spend`, `settlementState:
  not_applicable`, `payoutClaimAllowed: false`). The one-command install
  instinct survived as `npm install -g @openagentsinc/khala` and
  `khala fleet connect`.
- **The earning-modes taxonomy is half real.** "Earn Bitcoin in multiple
  different ways" anticipated training rewards, inference sales, forum
  activity, and coding work in one node. Today the Bitcoin rails exist
  (BOLT12 tipping with settled receipts, treasury wallet, MDK bridge) but are
  not P0. Marketplace/pooled resale is out of scope, and owner-local
  subscription capacity is never resold. The only live "mode" is own-capacity
  coding work that closes with no spend at all.
- **The contributor-count race was superseded by reliability gates.** Beating
  Bittensor's 200 contributors is a scale metric. Revision 31's P0 is
  reliable Desktop/mobile coding and fleet software behind gates R0–R7. The
  open live proof today is #8640 — one session, multiple providers, exact
  rows — not a contributor leaderboard.
- **The Percepta direction is preserved as research, not product.** The
  executor-class thesis got its full treatment in 237 and the
  `docs/tassadar/` essays. Nothing in the active plan implements it.

## 3. What to extract and carry forward

- **One install, many earning lanes.** The product framing — a single node
  binary whose value grows as more work types route through it — is the
  durable idea, and it survived structurally: Pylon's capability and counted
  capacity refs (`capacity.coding.codex.available=N`) are exactly a typed
  registry of "ways this node can work." If paid modes return, they slot into
  that same projection rather than a parallel one.
- **The pretrain-vs-finetune deliberation.** The episode records that the
  from-scratch-vs-Llama-finetune question was live right up to launch. Any
  future training revival should reopen it against `docs/tassadar/` rather
  than inheriting the launch-weekend answer.
- **Naming discipline.** Tassadar was named before it ran, and the name now
  denotes a retired lane. Names announced ahead of proof accumulate as
  vocabulary debt — the same lesson 237's "first and last human-shipped
  release" teaches at larger scale.
- **The date-first announcement as arc context.** 236 exists so 237 could
  land on a deadline. Read together they document the cost of a launch-driven
  cadence — useful memory for any post-R7 return to public launch events.

## 4. How to make it come true

Nothing from this episode is active work in its original form. Its claims
resolve through descendants and one dormant gate.

1. **Node software people actually install (active descendant).** The honest
   successor to "install our node software" is the fleet onboarding path:
   `khala fleet connect` bringing outside users' Codex accounts into isolated
   homes. Acceptance: an outside user's fleet completes real assignments
   live-proven with accepted no-spend closeouts — the modern form of a
   contributor joining the network.
2. **Multiple capacity under one owner (active).** #8640 (live simultaneous
   Codex+Claude proof) and #8636 (hybrid routing) carry the many-workers
   claim at honest scale. Acceptance: live-proven parallel execution with
   reconciled per-provider `token_usage_events` rows.
3. **Paid contribution modes (long-horizon).** Bitcoin-paid work of any kind
   re-enters only behind an explicit owner decision on marketplace scope,
   with the no-resale invariant for subscription accounts never waivable, and
   no settlement-bearing flow before the underlying work path is
   owner-accepted on own-capacity.
4. **The training run itself (dormant, owner-decision gate).** Reviving
   Tassadar is a new decision against the retained research record, not a
   resumption — and per 237's own flywheel logic, it follows a reliable
   acceptance substrate, never precedes it.

Each descendant graduates independently: code-landed → fixture-proven →
deployed → live-proven → owner-accepted → closed. The episode set a date. It
did not reach a rung.

## 5. Risks and tensions

- **Date-driven scope.** Announcing "Monday" in public compresses the
  pretrain-vs-finetune decision, the Pylon v0.3 packaging, and the payment
  wiring into a weekend. The retirement of the lane three weeks later is the
  measured cost of that compression. The current gate-driven cadence (R0–R7,
  CUT-01..27) is the correction.
- **Contributor count as vanity metric.** "Blow past 200 contributors" counts
  installs, not accepted work. The house metric vocabulary that followed —
  accepted closeouts, exact rows, "counter movement is never proof" — exists
  precisely because a contributor number can rise while nothing verifiable is
  produced.
- **Earning promises ahead of rails.** "Get paid Bitcoin to contribute" was
  copy before the payout path had proof. Today's posture inverts it: the
  rails exist quietly (settled BOLT12 receipts), and earning claims stay out
  of product copy until a settlement-bearing flow passes the rungs.
- **Retired-noun leakage.** Tassadar, Psionic, and Pylon-as-training-node are
  this episode's whole vocabulary. Quote it as the setup beat for 237 and the
  origin of the node-software framing — never as current product description.
