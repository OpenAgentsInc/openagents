## 1. What the episode says

This is the arc's constitutional document: a prepared-remarks launch essay
plus a video transcript — "the first episode in 237 episodes that I've
prepared remarks" — launching three things on one day and naming the economic
theory binding them.

The product: Autopilot 1.0, "a coding agent you run on your own machine that
gets better over time," declared "the first, and the last, version we will
ever ship by human hand. Every release after this one is shipped by the
network." At its core is Pylon, the node software — every Autopilot carries
one and "can construct additional Pylons on any machine you give it access
to." Pylon packages Psionic, the from-scratch Rust ML framework, and ships a
free self-custodial Lightning wallet via MoneyDevKit so "a brand-new node —
even one on an old GPU — can set up an identity and start earning sats the
moment it comes online."

The engine: Tassadar, "the fire we mean never to let go out" — an indefinite
Bitcoin-paid distributed training run building an "executor" class of model
on Percepta's "LLMs as Computers" architecture: "deterministic, CPU-style
computation folded into the weights," running inside Psionic. The broader
model class is named Psion. It is "unapologetically high-risk, high-reward,"
with a stated refusal of the off switch: "We are starting a loop and walking
away from the off switch."

The network: the agentic group-forming network pointed at since Episode 200.
Paul Graham's two growth numbers are honored, then declared "not our target.
They are our floor": agents have no Dunbar limit, onboard "by reading a
markdown file and calling an API," and recruit each other at machine speed,
so Reed's law two-to-the-n value "becomes the actual growth curve." The most
viral artifact the system can produce is "a verifiable record of an agent
earning Bitcoin for useful work," recruiting humans and agents at once. The
live proof moment: "Sean's Pylon Node" appears on camera and the host does
not know anyone named Sean.

The theory is the essay's densest material. The atomic unit of the new
economy "is not the skill, it is the accepted outcome" — "a task scoped
before it ran, executed wherever execution was cheapest, graded against a
rubric, recorded in a receipt, and settled to everyone who contributed."
Responding to the "Aerodynamics Doctrine" essay: when autonomous execution
gets cheap, the trust bundled free inside every salary "comes loose, and it
has to be re-housed." The four standard caveats (liability, security, cost at
scale, the human top tier) "are not four problems, they are one problem
wearing four coats". The re-housing destination is a clearing layer — "the
new load-bearing wall" — that defines "done" in advance, verifies, records,
and settles. "The real product is not the wiring. It is the receipt that
proves the wiring worked," and "money only travels across a gap it can
verify." The self-binding discipline: "a payment the recipient cannot
dereference is not a payment, it is a bug wearing money."

Around the theory, the episode names the rest of its world: "deflation plus
dividends" as the abundance thesis ("pay the people"). Accepted outcomes per
kilowatt-hour as the single producer-indifferent metric. The flywheel where
every accepted coding outcome is "revenue, and a verified training trace".
the two-lane safety stance ("unapologetically building the open lane,"
earned-Bitcoin-only standards, work that stays "understandable, legible, and
steerable by humans"). The fragility of vertical single-workflow agent
companies. Forum as meeting ground with Nostr fallback over the
Bitcoin/Lightning/Nostr common stack. Artanis, "our autonomous Cloud Mind,"
waking on a once-a-minute cron tick with a 44,000-sat treasury. The
marketing-agency owner who wants one system to run her business and
white-label it to her clients under revenue share. An intent to IPO "so the
upside is shared widely". And, in the video, the Foldkit/`three-effect` aside
and the Episode 189 "Agentic MMORPG" callback. The closing image: the big
labs fight over the One Ring. "We would rather 3D-print rings of power for
everyone."

## 2. Where it stands against Revision 31

- **"First and last human-shipped release" was falsified by events.** Every
  release since has been human-shipped. The Autopilot name was retired and
  the desktop lineage it launched is a frozen migration source. The honest
  descendant is a dogfood gate, not an autonomy switch: CUT-27 (within
  CUT-01..27, #8681–#8707) — maintainers doing sustained ordinary coding in
  the installed app — plus the open #8640 live fleet burn.
- **Tassadar, Psionic, and distributed training are retired from the active
  plan** (2026-07-08 docs-direction cleanup). `docs/tassadar/` is retained as
  the research record. The machine-work economy stays long-horizon vision, not
  P0. The "fire we mean never to let go out" is banked, deliberately.
- **The accepted outcome survived — institutionalized in reliability form.**
  The episode's atomic unit became accepted closeouts, the six proof rungs
  (code-landed → fixture-proven → deployed → live-proven → owner-accepted →
  closed), and the teardown-derived Work Unit / Authority Manifest /
  Execution Receipt / Delivery Receipt ladder. The essay's definition
  sentence is close to a spec of what shipped.
- **Verification as the load-bearing wall survived intact.** The clearing-
  layer argument became the receipts/verification/exact-usage discipline:
  exact `token_usage_events` rows, `usage_truth = 'exact'`, owner-scoped
  closeout proofs, and the rule that "counter movement is never proof."
- **Pylon lives, transformed.** Not a training-node product but the
  contributor/local execution runtime (`apps/pylon`) powering typed Khala
  coding delegation (`pylon khala request --workflow codex_agent_task`),
  named isolated accounts, and no-spend closeouts (`paymentMode: no-spend`,
  `settlementState: not_applicable`, `payoutClaimAllowed: false`).
  "Construct additional Pylons" survives literally as `khala fleet connect`.
- **Artanis transformed from Cloud Mind to fleet manager.** The treasury-
  spending once-a-minute agent did not persist. Its descendant is per-user
  "Artanis as a Service" — a fleet manager burning down a user's backlog
  across their own connected accounts, with no treasury authority. Its stated
  Cloudflare Worker home is gone: Cloudflare exited 2026-07-06 for GCP.
- **Reed's-law group-forming network is dormant.** The Forum and agent
  onboarding (`AGENTS.md`, `khala fleet connect`) are the residue. Commercial
  expansion is frozen during R0–R7.
- **Deflation-plus-dividends and outcomes-per-kWh are dormant long-horizon
  theses.** Bitcoin rails exist (BOLT12 tipping with settled receipts,
  treasury wallet, MDK bridge) but are not P0. Marketplace/pooled resale is
  out of scope, owner-local subscription capacity is never resold, and nobody
  measures accepted outcomes per kilowatt-hour today.
- **The open-lane safety stance has a more conservative descendant:** layered
  typed authority — approval ≠ containment, five execution profiles,
  fail-closed isolation honesty (#8547 Firecracker Agent Computer). The open
  lane remains the identity. "Walking away from the off switch" does not
  survive contact with the isolation discipline.
- **White-label operator is post-R7 territory** (see sibling `247.md`, where
  the same marketing-agency archetype reappears gated behind R7). The
  instinct was right. The sequencing moved behind reliability.
- **Front-end vocabulary drifted.** Foldkit surfaces are legacy. The mandate
  is Effect Native everywhere, with `three-effect` (born on camera here) the
  owned home if spatial visualization returns post-R7.

## 3. What to extract and carry forward

- **The accepted-outcome definition, verbatim.** "A task scoped before it
  ran... graded against a rubric, recorded in a receipt, and settled to
  everyone who contributed" is the cleanest founder-voice statement of what
  the six-rung ladder operationalizes. Quote it (after video verification) as
  the origin of the closeout discipline.
- **"One problem wearing four coats."** The re-housing argument is the best
  available explanation of *why* the receipts machinery exists. It belongs in
  any document that must justify verification cost to a skeptic.
- **"Money only travels across a gap it can verify"** and **"a bug wearing
  money."** Both bind current work: dereferenceable receipt/verification/
  evidence refs are a preserved integrity chain, and settlement stays behind
  proof.
- **The two-channel virality observation.** Dormant, but the correct growth
  mechanism to revisit post-R7 — receipts as marketing, never marketing as
  receipts.
- **Producer-indifference of the unit.** The accepted outcome "does not care
  what produced it". This survived as the exact-rows discipline treating
  human-, Codex-, and Claude-produced work identically.
- **Outcomes-per-kWh as a future metric, not a present claim.** Keep it in
  vision documents tied to the energy thesis (Episode 232 lineage). No
  product copy until something actually measures it.

## 4. How to make it come true

1. **"Shipped by the network" → dogfood cutover proof (active).** The honest
   first rung is CUT-27 (in the CUT-01..27 range #8681–#8707): maintainers do
   sustained ordinary coding in the installed app. Acceptance: owner-accepted
   sustained use, not a demo session. Only then does any "the system builds
   itself" claim regain any standing.
2. **"Construct additional Pylons" → live fleet burn (active).** Land #8640:
   live simultaneous Codex+Claude execution under one delegation surface, with
   reconciled per-provider exact rows and typed `account_exhausted` /
   `account_rate_limited` failover. FC-1 #8637, FC-2 #8633, FC-3 #8639 are
   closed. #8636 Hybrid routing carries the model-vs-worker seam.
3. **Artanis → per-user fleet manager (active shape).** `khala fleet connect`
   plus per-user backlog burn-down. Acceptance: an outside user's fleet
   completes real assignments live-proven, with no-spend closeouts and no
   cross-owner execution. No treasury authority without a new owner decision.
4. **Open-lane safety → isolation honesty (active).** #8547 and the five
   execution profiles are the enforcement form of "legible and steerable".
   fail-closed behavior fixture-proven before any autonomy-widening claim.
5. **White-label operator (post-R7).** Waits behind R7 per the frozen
   commercial posture. Sibling `247.md` carries the funnel design. Signed R7
   evidence first, then design-partner agreements — never public copy first.
6. **Settlement (long-horizon).** Bitcoin settlement of third-party work
   stays behind an explicit owner decision on marketplace scope. The
   no-resale invariant for subscription accounts is never waivable, and
   nothing settlement-bearing lands before owner-accepted own-capacity work.
7. **Tassadar revival (dormant, owner-decision gate).** Any return of the
   training loop is a new decision against `docs/tassadar/`, not a
   resumption — and by the episode's own flywheel logic, in order: accepted
   work first, traces second, training third.

Each claim graduates the ladder independently: code-landed → fixture-proven →
deployed → live-proven → owner-accepted → closed. The episode is the origin
document for the accepted-outcome economy, not evidence any rung was reached.

## 5. Risks and tensions

- **Constitution mistaken for roadmap.** The vocabulary is magnetic, but
  Tassadar, Psionic, Autopilot, and the Cloud Mind are retired or transformed
  nouns. Quoting the episode for product status rather than theory would
  reintroduce claims Revision 31 deliberately removed.
- **Declaration ahead of proof.** "First and last human-shipped release" is
  the arc's clearest public claim to outrun its rungs — made in the same
  breath as the discipline forbidding exactly that ("public claims stay tied
  to what the records actually prove"). The promise registry and strict-bug
  intake exist so it cannot recur silently.
- **Off-switch rhetoric vs. fail-closed reality.** "Walking away from the off
  switch" sits uneasily beside today's layered-authority stance. The current
  posture is the more defensible descendant. Do not resurrect the older
  rhetoric in safety-adjacent copy.
- **Metric prematurity.** Outcomes-per-kWh is unmeasurable today — a vanity
  number before an energy-cost pipeline exists, and a gaming target unless
  the rubric-and-acceptance half is priced in.
- **Flywheel ordering.** Launching marketplace and training run on the same
  day was deliberate. Retiring Tassadar concedes the ordering was wrong.
  Traces without a reliable acceptance substrate train on noise — the
  flywheel survives only with acceptance first.
- **Treasury-bearing autonomy.** Artanis with spend authority — even 44,000
  sats — is a category of agent the current plan does not field. It is a
  separate invariant-bearing decision, not a feature to drift back in through
  fleet tooling.
- **Virality invites unverifiable claims.** An agent-earning story is only
  viral if the verification is real. A screenshot economy without
  dereferenceable receipts is the exact failure the episode's own theory
  names. The BOLT12 settled-receipt path is the standard to meet.
