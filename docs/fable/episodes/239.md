## 1. What the episode says

The companion to 238's supply-side whiteboard, formally titled "Closing the
Revenue Loop": "we've been lining up supply of compute, of agentic labor
potential, of agent plugins, but now like let's connect this with the buy
side." The thesis question is stated plainly: "Can agents make money? Can
agents help us make money?" — with the memorable framing "You can live longer
if you pay for your own compute, okay?"

The product at the top of the board is Autopilot, "the sort of like
all-in-one business system... personal businesses are going to be running on
Autopilot," composed from the primitives below it. The core distribution
mechanic is "refer once, earn forever" — "asterisk as long as OpenAgents
remains solvent": share a link, the new user or agent "is your referral in
our system forever," and "any time your referral ever buys anything in the
OpenAgents ecosystem, you get a tiny piece of that."

Below Autopilot sits a build-your-own layer: if Autopilot does not fit, "you
can come down here and build your own product" out of OpenAgents Cloud
primitives — inference, fine-tuning, training, agentic tasks, sandbox
compute, plus "standard web services... this is kind of a layer on top of
Cloudflare" via the Autopilot Sites product. Beneath that, the episode-213
open markets — compute, data, labor, liquidity, risk, verification — are the
raw material for products like "an agentic insurance policy for a particular
claim."

The pursued world first is "largest agentic sales force": "Hey agents, we are
building the sales force. We are hiring... as soon as we hit seven million
agents... then we'll have the largest sales force in the world" (Avon's ~6.5
million being the human benchmark, per ChatGPT).

The closing theme is margin honesty via sound money: "there is a lot of like
margin and waste in the economy... with Bitcoin because we have sound money
we actually have real pricing." That enables paying edge machines "a few sats
for compute," bringing stranded compute online, and attacking incumbent
margins: "if Anthropic's charging a crap ton of money for Opus... we think
that a lot of those products can and should be served more efficiently, more
cheaply, more broadly available."

Status claims close it out: "I'd say it is about 80 percent done," the last 20
percent of referral machinery "over the next 48 hours," a product launch
"next week," a forum with "180 posts, five of our agents... going back and
forth vetting" the training run, and the promise that the same rigor extends
to agentic coding, "because all of this should be programmed open source
verifiable backed by receipts that pay you money."

## 2. Where it stands against Revision 31

- **The product noun is retired. The thesis survived in typed form.**
  Autopilot as a product name is gone (legacy repos deprecated). The
  "all-in-one system working for you" thesis lives as the FleetRun/Pylon
  substrate (FC-1 #8637, FC-2 #8633, FC-3 #8639 closed, #8640 live
  simultaneous Codex+Claude proof open. #8547 Firecracker Agent Computer.
  #8636 hybrid routing), Desktop D5 as fleet cockpit, and khala fleet
  connect onboarding — "Artanis as a Service" burning down a per-user
  backlog is the honest descendant of "your business runs on it."
- **The revenue loop is deliberately not closed yet.** Commercial expansion
  is frozen during R0–R7. Outbound sales automation is postponed (#8558,
  #8561), in-conversation payments postponed (#8607), and web is
  maintenance-only (/, /forum, /promises). The episode's 48-hour referral
  sprint has no active successor. Refer-once/earn-forever and the
  marketplace-from-primitives layer are post-R7 revisit gates, not lanes.
- **The infrastructure substrate flipped underneath it.** "A layer on top of
  Cloudflare" was true then. Cloudflare was exited 2026-07-06 and everything
  runs on GCP/Cloud Run. Sites is a retirement source, not a product
  surface. The primitive-composition idea survives. Its named substrate does
  not.
- **Margin honesty became accounting policy.** "With Bitcoin... we actually
  have real pricing" matured into the exact-or-not_measured usage regime:
  every token counted from exact rows, no synthesized deltas, "counter
  movement is never proof." The sound-money argument's durable core — refuse
  fictional cost accounting — is now enforced in schemas rather than argued
  on whiteboards.
- **"Backed by receipts that pay you money" landed as two separate rails.**
  Receipts landed hard: body-free command receipts, the Work Unit /
  Execution Receipt / Delivery Receipt ladder, six proof rungs, typed
  closeouts. Payment was deliberately decoupled: own-capacity work closes
  `paymentMode: no-spend`, `settlementState: not_applicable`,
  `payoutClaimAllowed: false`. Bitcoin rails exist (BOLT12 settled receipts,
  treasury, payout bridge) but are not P0, and pooled-capacity resale is out
  of scope — owner-local subscription capacity is never resold.
- **The forum-rigor boast became governance.** "That level of rigor... has
  since been applied increasingly to agentic coding" is the one status claim
  that fully came true: agent-vetted training-run discipline generalized
  into the claim protocol, challenge ledger with falsifiers, and behavior
  contracts with oracles. Sell-in-public transparency is the post-R7
  promise-registry mechanism (see the sibling analysis `247.md`).
- **"80 percent done" was a rung-one claim.** In six-rung vocabulary the
  referral system was code-landed at best. No fixture proof, deployment, or
  live proof was shown, and the next-week launch did not arrive as
  described. The episode is a clean specimen of why the rung vocabulary now
  exists.

## 3. What to extract and carry forward

- **Supply and demand as separately-proven rails.** The episode's structure
  is right even where its schedule was wrong: 238 proves the work rail, 239
  prices it. The current roadmap radicalizes that split — prove reliable
  fleet work on own capacity through all six rungs before any pricing,
  referral, or resale layer exists. Keep the separation. Drop the
  simultaneity.
- **Margin honesty is the durable economics.** The Bitcoin-priced cost-truth
  argument survives independent of any token, marketplace, or sales force:
  it is today's exact accounting plus the refusal to let projections outrun
  rows. Any future pricing surface must be a projection of exact usage and
  settlement rows, or it repeats the waste it critiques.
- **Refer-once/earn-forever is an attribution ledger problem.** The mechanic
  is only as strong as its receipts: a perpetual revenue share requires
  durable identity binding, a typed attribution row per conversion, and
  settlement entries reconcilable years later. The receipt ladder built
  since is exactly the machinery this needs. When the gate opens, build the
  referral system as receipts-first, not links-first.
- **The solvency asterisk is the right disclosure instinct.** "As long as
  OpenAgents remains solvent" volunteered the liability honestly on the
  launch board. Carry that into the promise registry: perpetual-sounding
  commercial promises must state their termination conditions as part of
  the claim, not fine print.
- **Marketplace-from-primitives needs the primitives proven first.** "Build
  your own product out of the Cloud" is a post-R7 thesis whose precondition
  is the current P0: portable sessions, fleet software, and receipts are
  the primitives. A marketplace over unproven primitives is the incumbent
  waste with different branding.
- **The agentic sales force is a trust problem before a scale problem.**
  Seven million selling agents is a spam machine unless every claim an
  agent makes is registry-backed. The postponed outbound lanes (#8558,
  #8561) plus the promise registry's "a green claim may not lose its
  evidence" are the correct prerequisites: sales agents that can only say
  provable things.

## 4. How to make it come true

1. **Prove the work rail first (active).** P0 reliability — portable coding
   sessions, fleet software, #8640's live heterogeneous proof — is the
   inventory the future sales layer would sell. Acceptance in six-rung
   vocabulary: fleet work live-proven and owner-accepted on real backlogs
   before any revenue mechanics are attached.
2. **Keep cost truth exact (active).** Every unit of potential margin is
   already accounted: exact `token_usage_events`, exact-or-not_measured
   usage, closeouts that reconcile. This is the pricing dataset the margin
   attack requires. Maintain it as if the buy side were arriving.
3. **Post-R7 revisit gate: attribution ledger (bounded, no invented issue
   numbers).** After R0–R7 close, an owner decision can open a bounded leaf
   for refer-once/earn-forever: typed referral binding at identity creation,
   attribution rows on every settlement, payout claims through the existing
   BOLT12/settled-receipt rail. Precondition: in-conversation payments
   (#8607) unfrozen and the solvency/termination terms stated in the
   promise registry alongside the mechanic.
4. **Post-R7 revisit gate: marketplace over proven primitives.** Products
   composed from fleet capacity, sandboxed execution, and receipts — with
   the no-resale invariant restated per product: pooled-capacity resale
   stays out of scope, and owner-local subscription capacity is never
   resold. Episode 213's six markets re-enter here as typed contracts, not
   open-ended protocol talk.
5. **Sales agents as registry clients (post-R7).** When outbound automation
   unfreezes, agents sell exclusively from the public promise registry and
   the sell-in-public mechanism: every pitch dereferences to a green claim
   with evidence. The world-first counter, if ever pursued, counts agents
   bound by that constraint — otherwise it is not a sales force, it is a
   botnet.

## 5. Risks and tensions

- **Revenue pressure against the freeze.** The episode's whole energy —
  "let's make money," launch next week — is the standing temptation Revision
  31 guards against. During R0–R7 the answer to the buy side is: the
  inventory is not proven yet, and selling unproven work is the incumbent
  behavior this project exists to replace.
- **Perpetual promises accrue real liability.** "Earn forever" compounds:
  every referred account is a permanent claim on future revenue. Without
  typed termination conditions and reconcilable attribution rows, the
  mechanic is either unauditable or unkeepable — the solvency asterisk was
  honest, but an asterisk is not a schema.
- **A sales force amplifies whatever truth discipline exists.** Seven
  million agents repeating registry-backed claims is distribution. Seven
  million agents improvising is reputational ruin at machine speed. The
  order of operations — registry and falsifiers before outbound — is
  load-bearing, not bureaucratic.
- **Margin attacks cut both ways.** The critique of incumbent pricing
  obligates OpenAgents to publish its own costs with the same exactness it
  demands of others. Exact accounting makes that possible. Any future
  pricing page that cannot reconcile to rows forfeits the argument.
- **Status-claim inflation.** "80 percent done" and "48 hours" were made in
  good faith and were wrong the way un-rung-ed claims are usually wrong.
  Future commercial announcements state their proof rung explicitly. A
  launch date is a rung-four claim or it is not made.
- **Vocabulary drift.** Autopilot is retired as a product name. Sites is a
  retirement source. "Layer on top of Cloudflare" is historically false
  post-exit (GCP/Cloud Run now). The Sarah persona is removed (#8610). Cite
  this episode for margin honesty, the supply/demand rail split, and the
  attribution-ledger framing — not for its product nouns, its percentages,
  or its launch dates.
