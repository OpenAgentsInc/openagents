## 1. What the episode says

Episode 242 is the Khala launch announcement: "the new flagship product of
OpenAgents," described as "Collective Intelligence, behind a free API." It
positions Khala as OpenAgents' answer to the model-orchestrator wave —
OpenRouter's Fusion and Sakana's Fugu are named directly — and stakes the
differentiation on four contrasts with Sakana's version of collective
intelligence: engineered vs. "grown, like an ecology". Designed-and-searched vs.
"emerge from markets — bottom-up instead of top-down". "Graded on its own
benchmarks" vs. "selected by bitcoin-paid verified value". Closed pool vs. open
pool.

The concrete product shape: an OpenAI-compatible endpoint at
`openagents.com/api`, live at recording time, one model called Khala ("we may
split that up into different versions of Khala, but for now it is just sort of
one model"), fully open source ("if you want to clone our repo and run all of
this on your own infrastructure, you are welcome to do that"), with a free
"limited research preview" and paid tiers to come. Behind the endpoint sits a
router fanning into a network of composable plugins — the Khala, named for the
Protoss telepathic link — whose output "could be anything": text, code, full
software, a website deployment, a legal brief, a research paper.

The Fugu comparison produces the episode's stated "lightbulb moment": any
long-running process can live behind this API shape if the UI streams events and
supports interruption and steering. That folds in the standing market thesis —
Pylon Network nodes contributing compute, data, or labor, "with any paid value
distributed fairly to whoever contributes pieces of the workflow." Architecture
claims: each plugin is a Blueprint/DSPy signature, independently optimizable via
GEPA at the prompt level. Traces will be visible on the website, with privacy as
a paid option. Confidential compute via Google Cloud is available as a node
type. The economic claim: unlike "massive pre-trained models — Opus, Fable —
that depreciate rapidly," Khala "does not depreciate. This actually improves,"
with contributors "paid proportional to any paid usage." The episode closes by
promising demos, code, and live debugging "even if this is not fully 100%
implemented yet."

## 2. Where it stands against Revision 31

The core API-shape thesis came true and hardened. The OpenAI-compatible Khala
gateway is landed and closed as FC-BRAIN #8600, with exact receipts,
VAD-fragment coalescing, session/turn caps, and typed fallback — i.e., the
launch-episode surface now carries the governance the launch only gestured at.
The public tokens-served counter the episode's go-to-market implied exists as
the `khala-tokens-served` endpoint, under the explicit discipline that "counter
movement is never proof" — every public number reconciles to exact
`token_usage_events` rows.

The "may split into different versions" aside resolved in the opposite direction
and is now settled vocabulary: Khala is a single model surface
(`openagents/khala`). `khala-mini`/`pro`/`code` are deprecated names (Episode
243 spent live debugging time on exactly that stale-slug failure). Naming also
tightened: "Khala" is the collective-intelligence product and persona-neutral
inference/routing engine. The owned sync engine is strictly the two-word "Khala
Sync."

The contribution-economy half is direction, not status. What shipped is the
own-capacity form of it: typed Khala coding delegation through owner-linked
Pylons (`pylon khala request --workflow codex_agent_task`,
`khala fleet connect`, isolated accounts under
`<pylon home>/accounts/codex/<ref>`), with exact per-turn token rows and
owner-only traces. The open pooled marketplace, three-way paid splits, and
contributor payouts from Khala serving remain explicitly ungreen.
marketplace/pooled-capacity resale is out of scope for the current own-capacity
lanes, and owner-local subscription capacity is never resold. The
Blueprint/DSPy/GEPA plugin-ecology framing is historical vocabulary: today's
Khala routes across provider lanes and the fleet substrate rather than a
registry of independently optimized DSPy signatures, and Blueprint is deprecated
source material. Confidential-compute nodes and paid trace-privacy tiers remain
unbuilt direction.

Proof-rung read: gateway and counter are live-proven and closed (#8600). Typed
own-capacity delegation is live-proven with receipts. Marketplace, contributor
settlement, and self-improvement claims sit below code-landed — direction with
no promise.

## 3. What to extract and carry forward

- **Openness as the differentiation, kept honest.** The Fugu contrast — open
  pool, inspectable pipeline, selection by paid verified value rather than
  self-graded benchmarks — is the durable positioning. It now has teeth the
  episode lacked: the six proof rungs, the exact-or-`not_measured` usage rule,
  and counter-to-ledger reconciliation are what "selected by verifiable value"
  means operationally. Carry the contrast forward as governance, not just
  rhetoric.
- **Any long-running process behind one typed surface.** The episode's lightbulb
  — streamed events, interruption, steering over a 15-minute job — is now the
  teardown-derived canon: one engine behind a versioned typed protocol
  (Thread→Turn→Item + Work Unit/Receipt), durable admission before execution,
  steer-vs-queue delivery, three read surfaces. Episode 242 named the
  requirement. The architecture docs now specify it.
- **Watchable work.** The instinct to make Khala's activity publicly visible
  (counter, traces, the later Verse fanout idea) survives as the public
  projection layer — bounded projections of exact rows, never synthesized
  numbers.
- **"Improves, does not depreciate."** This claim is only redeemable through a
  recurring evaluation loop. It maps to the gym/Eval Suite direction: repeatable
  benchmarks with an owner-armed real lane, fixture runs labeled illustrative,
  cost-per-accepted-outcome as the axis.
- **Market framing routed through own-capacity first.** "Paid fairly for what
  you contribute" survives as the sequence: owner-local fleet capacity with
  exact receipts now, settlement-bearing pooled capacity only after owner-armed
  settlement exists.

## 4. How to make it come true

The path runs through the fleet substrate, not a plugin registry. FC-1 #8637
(durable FleetRun authority), FC-2 #8633 (standing mixed Codex/Claude/Grok Pylon
supervisor with one claim registry), and FC-3 #8639
(supervision/steer/approval/receipts) are the current carriers of "many
contributors composing work behind one endpoint," restricted to owner-linked
capacity. #8640 (live simultaneous Codex+Claude proof) is the open gate that
turns the episode's ensemble claim into a live-proven receipt.

Bounded next leaves, described without inventing issue numbers: (1) a public
routing-inspectability page that shows, for a served request, which lane handled
it and reconciles to the exact `token_usage_events` row — the honest form of
"you should never have a question what OpenAgents is doing". (2) An Eval Suite
that operationalizes "improves, does not depreciate" as a dated, repeatable
quality curve for `openagents/khala`, fixture lane deterministic and labeled,
real lane owner-armed. (3) A written settlement precondition doc for any future
contributor-payout promise, so the marketplace half of 242 has a defined
owner-flip rather than ambient aspiration.

Acceptance in six-rung vocabulary: the gateway is already closed. The
inspectability and eval leaves would each need to climb code-landed →
fixture-proven → deployed → live-proven before any public claim. Contributor
payment from Khala serving stays labeled direction until owner-armed settlement
takes it to owner-accepted.

## 5. Risks and tensions

- **Do not resurrect model-variant naming.** The episode's own "we may split
  Khala into versions" is the one line that aged into a deprecated pattern.
  `khala-mini` cost live debugging time in 243. Single model surface, full stop.
- **Ecology rhetoric outrunning receipts.** "Grown like an ecology" and
  "selected by bitcoin-paid verified value" describe a settlement loop that does
  not exist yet. Public copy must stay inside the promise registry. The 243 GTM
  doc's own honesty bar (no claimed contributor payouts absent owner-armed
  settlement) governs.
- **The free-data quid pro quo needs promise-registry treatment.** The launch
  framing implies free-tier traffic funds training and derived insights earn
  users Bitcoin. Neither is a green promise. Neither belongs in copy until it
  is.
- **Blueprint/DSPy vocabulary is historical.** Carrying "each plugin is a DSPy
  signature" forward would misdescribe the shipped router and reintroduce
  deprecated architecture language.
- **Marketplace scope discipline.** The open-pool ambition must not leak into
  current own-capacity lanes: subscription-account capacity is never resold, and
  pooled/third-party settlement stays out of scope until explicitly opened.
- **Depreciation claim without a curve is vibes.** Until the gym produces dated
  head-to-head numbers, "improves over time" is direction, not a measurement —
  never publish it as one.
