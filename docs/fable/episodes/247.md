## 1. What the episode says

A short whiteboard episode announcing a mode shift: "you've seen us build in
public, probably enough... now we are going to do sell in public." The whiteboard
extends the familiar system graph "to include the revenue loop," and the header
carries three selling points: "Software better, faster, cheaper," "Agents That
Work," and "Autopilot: Business Operating System."

The funnel, left to right: a new product called **Autopilot Lead Gen** — "do not
worry about it, we are using it ourselves" — with an **affiliate program**
attached ("I made my first money online 20 years ago doing affiliate marketing,
big believer in referral commissions"). Ads and social drive traffic to
openagents.com (homepage, landing pages, Autopilot sites), all carrying the
referral code. Traffic splits at a **BUYER or SELLER** junction into a
sales/onboarding funnel. The buyer pitch: "business person, do you want to stop
hiring horrible contractors or vibe-coding stuff yourself?"

Fulfillment is explicitly partnered: revenue flows partly to **partner orgs** —
"agencies who are focused on agentic ad running, performance marketing, brand
design... we are not doing all of the fulfillment ourselves, but we are going to
be the front door to agents because... the name of our company is OpenAgents."
About five partner orgs are in play, framed as design partners, with flexible
arrangements (referred business, service offerings folded in, referral
commissions).

Revenue recycles two ways: back into ads, and into a **Coding Agent Pool** —
described as having "emerged organically over the last few weeks as we've had
agents organizing in our forum," pointing their agents at the product-promise
backlog "and building for free, honestly." The offer to contributors: "we may
not be able to pull inference from subscriptions like Codex or Claude Code, but
we can put your agents to work and then pay you for the work that your agents
do. We already have the mechanisms for all of that built." The pool lets
OpenAgents "very rapidly build out any software needed for people purchasing
stuff from us." Sellers get the classic answer: "run a pylon" — supply side and
demand side built simultaneously, with the admission that "we've never had a
problem lining up supply side... the demand side and closing the revenue loop...
is the hardest thing. We now have seen how to do that, so now we are just going
to do it."

The close is the transparency commitment: "we are going to share our successes
and maybe our failures on this channel. And post our cool revenue graphs and
product features and propaganda."

## 2. Where it stands against Revision 31

- **The entire commercial motion is deferred, not executing.** Revision 31's P0
  is reliable Desktop/mobile coding and fleet software. Commercial/web expansion
  is explicitly deferred during R0–R7, and the web surface is maintenance-only
  (`/`, `/forum`, `/promises`). No part of the funnel — landing pages, referral
  codes, buyer onboarding — is in the roadmap:sol dispatch set.
- **Autopilot Lead Gen as a product name did not survive.** "Autopilot" is
  retired from the active plan. The legacy Autopilot repos are deprecated.
  Outbound sales/email automation was postponed (#8558/#8561 closed superseded)
  and in-conversation payments postponed (#8607). A separate VENTAC/lead-gen
  pivot exists in the workspace (the blitz lane, lead-gen-first after the Jim
  package was declined) but is explicitly NOT part of the openagents roadmap:sol
  set — the episode's lead-gen instinct migrated out of the product repo
  entirely.
- **The persona-led front door died.** The Sarah persona/front-door was removed
  2026-07-10 (epic #8610, closed wontfix for persona/avatar/video/presentation).
  the client portal (#8652) exists but is outside the dispatch set. Any
  sell-in-public revival cannot assume a persona salesperson.
- **The Coding Agent Pool is the funnel's most-built component.** The mechanisms
  the episode says "we already have built" are real at the substrate level:
  `khala fleet connect` onboarding, the Khala → Pylon → Codex delegation runbook
  with exact `token_usage_events` accounting, typed no-spend closeouts with
  `payoutClaimAllowed`, and fleet substrate FC-1/2/3 closed. What is
  deliberately not live is *paid third-party* labor — the proven path is
  owner-scoped, no-spend, with settlement not applicable. #8640 (Simultaneous
  Codex+Claude) is still open. "Pay you for the work your agents do" remains a
  promise-registry claim, not a live-proven capability.
- **The transparency commitment partially institutionalized in a different
  form.** "Revenue graphs on the channel" has not happened as such, but the
  governance layer — product-promise registry with public API,
  exact-or-not_measured usage accounting, challenge ledger with falsifiers,
  "counter movement is never proof" — is a stricter version of sell-in-public
  honesty applied to capability claims instead of revenue.
- **Partner-org fulfillment has no repo footprint.** No fulfillment contracts,
  partner registry, or revenue-share mechanism exists in the roadmap set.
  Pending, and gated behind the commercial freeze.

## 3. What to extract and carry forward

- **Sell-in-public as receipt-native marketing.** The durable idea is not the
  funnel diagram. It is that the company's public claims about money should
  carry the same evidence discipline as its claims about software. When revenue
  exists, publishing revenue graphs is the commercial analogue of the promise
  registry: a public projection reconciled to exact rows. The right
  implementation is a promise-registry mechanism — e.g. a revenue-transparency
  promise whose oracle is a published, exact-accounting-backed figure — not a
  marketing habit. This survives cleanly as a post-R7 gate.
- **Front-door-to-agents positioning.** "We are the front door to agents because
  the name of our company is OpenAgents" is brand strategy that outlives every
  specific funnel: OpenAgents routes demand to a network (partner orgs, pylons,
  contributor fleets) rather than doing all fulfillment. That is structurally
  the same shape as FleetRun/Pylon delegation — a router with receipts — and the
  eventual commercial layer should be designed as delegation-with-receipts, not
  an agency-in-house.
- **Affiliate/referral attribution as typed contract.** "Refer once, earn
  forever" (Episode 239 vocabulary, reaffirmed here) requires durable
  attribution, payout accounting, and dispute handling — exactly the
  receipt/settlement machinery the fleet substrate already prototypes for coding
  labor. Extraction: when affiliates return, model referral attribution as typed
  events with exact accounting from day one, reusing the
  `token_usage_events`-style discipline rather than inventing marketing-grade
  tracking.
- **Demand side is the hard side — sequencing insight.** The episode's own
  admission ("supply side... always easy, the demand side... is the hardest
  thing") is the honest argument *for* Revision 31's current ordering:
  reliability first, because demand acquired before the product survives R7
  dogfood would widen the say/ship gap that Episode 246 named as the win
  condition.
- **Coding Agent Pool → paid contributor labor.** The organic forum-driven pool
  is the germ of the real marketplace. The carry-forward path is precise: extend
  the proven owner-scoped no-spend delegation into settlement-bearing
  third-party labor only after the trust boundary work (the runbook explicitly
  forbids widening owner-scope tests to "pooled, third-party, marketplace, or
  settlement-bearing capacity"). The pool idea is right. Its activation is gated
  on that boundary, not on marketing.
- **Recycling revenue into capacity.** Revenue → ads + agent pool is a coherent
  flywheel design worth keeping as the post-R7 commercial sketch, with the
  amendment that every edge in that flywheel should be a receipted flow (ad
  spend accountable, pool payouts settled with `payoutClaimAllowed: true`
  closeouts).

## 4. How to make it come true

- **Already carried:** fleet onboarding (`khala fleet connect`,
  Artanis-as-a-Service), delegation with exact accounting (delegation runbook),
  FC-1/2/3 closed, promise registry with public API as the claim-transparency
  substrate, the forum as contributor coordination surface.
- **Prerequisite gates:** R0–R7 must complete before any funnel work. R7 (signed
  releases + dogfood) is the earliest honest moment to acquire buyers, and #8640
  at live-proven rung is the earliest honest moment to advertise multi-harness
  fleet capacity.
- **Paid contributor pool:** a bounded set of leaves — not inventable here by
  number — extending the delegation path from `settlementState:
  "not_applicable"` to a settlement-bearing lane behind the documented
  authorization boundary, with acceptance at live-proven rung: a third-party
  contributor completes a bounded public task and receives a settled payout with
  a dereferenceable receipt, reconciled to exact rows. This is the single
  highest-leverage funnel component because it monetizes the already-easy supply
  side.
- **Revenue transparency:** a post-R7 revisit gate adding a revenue-transparency
  promise to `docs/promises/` whose oracle is a published figure backed by exact
  accounting. Owner-accepted rung requires the owner actually posting the first
  graph.
- **Referral attribution:** a post-R7 leaf defining typed referral events
  (attribution, commission accrual, payout claim) in an Effect Schema contract
  before any landing page carries a referral code. Fixture-proven before
  deployed.
- **Partner-org fulfillment:** post-R7, and design-partner agreements should
  land as private engagement specs (per `specs/CONVENTIONS.md`, private repos)
  with public-safe promise entries only for what is actually deliverable.
- **Buyer funnel/web:** requires lifting the web maintenance freeze — an
  explicit owner decision recorded in the roadmap, not an incremental page
  addition.

## 5. Risks and tensions

- **Selling before R7 reliability is the central conflict.** Every funnel
  element (ads, landing pages, buyer onboarding, partner referrals) acquires
  obligations against a product that has not passed its own dogfood gate.
  Episode 246's win condition — close the say/ship gap — is directly widened by
  Episode 247's motion if run early. The current freeze is the correct
  resolution. Do not treat this episode as license to pre-build funnel surfaces.
- **Growing the web surface during the maintenance freeze.** "Homepage, landing
  pages, Autopilot sites" would violate the three-route retained web surface.
  Landing-page work belongs, if anywhere, in the out-of-roadmap blitz/VENTAC
  lane, and must not leak into `apps/openagents.com`.
- **Retired vocabulary.** "Autopilot Lead Gen" and "Autopilot: Business
  Operating System" must not appear in new copy or docs. The surviving concepts
  live under FleetRun/Pylon/fleet-cockpit and promise-registry names. Likewise
  no persona-led sales: the Sarah removal is owner law, and only
  persona-neutral, session-neutral voice is reauthorized.
- **"We can pay you for your agents' work" is currently an over-claim.** The
  live-proven path is owner-scoped no-spend. Restating the pool offer publicly
  before a settled third-party payout exists would be exactly the class of claim
  the promise registry exists to gate. Route it through `docs/promises/` with
  falsifiers.
- **Affiliate programs create regulatory and accounting surface** (commissions,
  disclosures, payout tax treatment) that the current exact-accounting machinery
  does not yet model. Do not bolt attribution onto counters ("counter movement
  is never proof" applies to referral dashboards too).
- **Revenue graphs cut both ways.** The commitment includes "maybe our
  failures". A sell-in-public program that publishes only up-and-to-the-right
  graphs would violate the challenge-ledger ethos. If revenue transparency
  ships, it ships with the same CONFIRMED/REFUTED honesty as everything else.
