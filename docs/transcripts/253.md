**[01:46]**
*[Visual: Christopher David puts his hands on his head in a gesture of stress/frustration.]*

**Christopher David:** *[In a mock stressed voice]* Ahhh! I don't trust you, okay? You get it, Anthropic? We don't trust Anthropic here.

*[Reading again]* "Node is an Impact Project hosted by the vendor-neutral OpenJS Foundation. A Node cutover would therefore remove Anthropic stewardship from the production runtime rather than merely rename it. Vite Plus would introduce Cloudflare stewardship in the development and build path, but that is a smaller and more reversible dependency. Its outputs are ordinary JavaScript assets, declarations, and package metadata. The underlying Vite, Vitest, Oxc, Rolldown, and tsdown pieces remain separately usable... That distinction is the best vendor-risk argument for Option B: production runtime governance moves to a neutral foundation; the corporate dependency is pushed outward to a replaceable toolchain layer."

Fine, good, great. Sounds good.
